Pronunciation
  1) A syllable is long if:     1) All vs are pronounced as "w."
 
a) it contains a long vowel or dipthong, or
      
 
b) it is followed by 2 + consonents.
     2) All cs are hard and therefore pronounced as "k."
         
  2) A syllable is short if it is not long.      
         
  3) The last syllable of a Latin word is called the ultima. The      
  next-to-the-last syllabele is called the penult . The      
  third-to-the-last syllable is called the antepenult.      
         
  4) If the penult is long, it receives the accent:      
 
sa|pi|en|tis, mo|vê|bat
      
         
  5) If the penult is short, the antepenult receives the accent:      
 
sa|pi|êns, mô|ve|rit
